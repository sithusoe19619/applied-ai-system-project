# Senior Pet Support

Senior pets often need lower-impact exercise, more frequent comfort checks, and closer observation of appetite, hydration, and mobility. Shorter walks, joint-friendly movement, and calm enrichment are often easier to tolerate than intense activity.

Older pets may benefit from routines that include medication reminders, slow feeding, and extra time for transitions. Owners should watch for changes in gait, confusion, appetite, and bathroom habits and report major changes to a veterinarian.

A senior pet plan should prioritize consistency over intensity. When time is limited, essential care such as feeding, hydration, medication reminders, and mobility support should be scheduled before optional enrichment.
