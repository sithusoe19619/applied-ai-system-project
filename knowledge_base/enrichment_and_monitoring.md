# Enrichment and Daily Monitoring

Daily care plans are stronger when they combine essential care with realistic enrichment and observation. Short play, sniff walks, brushing, or calm interaction can improve quality of life without requiring large blocks of time.

Observation tasks are useful for special-needs pets because they create a record of appetite, water intake, energy, and bathroom habits. These tasks should be short, specific, and easy to complete consistently.

When the day's schedule is tight, a planner should preserve high-priority essentials first and then fill remaining time with short enrichment or monitoring tasks that still fit the available budget.
