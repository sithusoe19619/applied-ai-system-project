# Medication Routine and Safety

Medication-related tasks in a home routine should focus on reminders, documentation, and observation, not dosage changes. A planning assistant can remind the owner to give medication exactly as already prescribed and to log whether the dose was completed.

If a medication is missed, vomited, or refused, owners should contact their veterinarian or follow the written veterinary plan instead of guessing a replacement dose. Human medications should not be substituted unless a veterinarian explicitly directed it.

Reliable medication plans include the scheduled time, a note to document completion, and a safety reminder not to adjust the dose independently.
